A Brief History...

The Monty Hall Problem is a statistical problem based off of a game played on an American TV show named "Lets Make a Deal", which aired during the 1960's and 70's. The problem actually gets its name from the host, Monty Hall (shown to the left). The problem was first answered by Marilyn vos Savant (shown to the right) in her weekly printed column of Parade titled "Ask Marilyn". Her answer sparked debate from thousands of readers, and was even featured in an article on the front page of The New York Times . Eventually it was proven that her original answer (which will be discussed below) was correct!

Abstract

Suppose you're on a game show, and you're given the choice of three doors. Behind one door is a car, behind the others, goats. You pick a door, say #1, and the host, who knows what's behind the doors, opens another door, say #3, which has a goat. He says to you, "Do you want to pick door #2?" Is it to your advantage to switch your choice of doors?
